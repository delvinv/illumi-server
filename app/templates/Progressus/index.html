ILLUMI: Ideas worth spreading

A critical look at research dissemination and tokenistic participation

CAN YOU HANDLE THE HEAT?



What is ILLUMI?

Issues with current research practices

ILLUMI came about through our discussions with older members of the public who were interested in deeper engagement with the university. These discussions revealed a deap seated frustration with the way research is currently undertaken, disseminated and participant recruitment.

Introducing ILLUMI

Following on from this and some other engagement activities, we designed ILLUMI. ILLUMI is a tool which allows researchers to spread their research ideas to a wider audience. But there is a twist: the researcher doesnt get to control who engages with their idea.

'Encouraging reflective practices'

The ILLUMI platform takes the researcher's idea and sends it to a randomly chosen participant (who are already set-up with ILLUMI and have received a client device). This participant can then engage and reflect with the research. Then, they send their reflections and thoughts back to the ILLUMI server.

Redefining 'impact'

The server then forwards these reflections to another participant, and so forth. This in effect creates a 'research chinese whispers'. The researcher can track the output of each of these reflections, and use that as a guide to see how impressionable and 'impactful' their research idea is.
